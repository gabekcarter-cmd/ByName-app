Backed by how memory actually works

Cognitive scientists call it spaced retrieval ‚Äî reviewing information at expanding intervals is one of the most effective ways to move it from short-term to long-term memory. Landauer & Bjork demonstrated this specifically with name recall in 1978, and it's been validated in over four decades of research since.

By Name's daily, weekly, and monthly review cycle maps directly to these research-backed intervals. You're not just scrolling through a list. You're training your brain to hold on.

Why it works

In 1978, researchers Landauer & Bjork tested this exact scenario ‚Äî remembering people's names ‚Äî and found that reviewing at expanding intervals dramatically outperformed cramming or re-reading. Later studies showed even patients with early Alzheimer's could retain names for weeks using this method. The principle is simple: review a name right before you'd forget it, then wait a little longer each time. That's exactly what By Name's Today, This Week, and This Month views are built to do.
